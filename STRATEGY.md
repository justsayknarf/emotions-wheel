# Emotions Wheel Strategy

## Target problem

People trying to build an emotional check-in habit face a precision trap: most tools require selecting one correct word, which implies feelings can be cleanly categorized. Nothing ever feels exactly right, so the habit doesn't stick. The underlying crux is that emotional vocabulary is underdeveloped — articulation is a muscle, and recall-from-scratch at the moment of check-in is the highest-friction point in the routine.

## Our approach

Record the coordinate in emotional space as the primary datum; treat words as optional vocabulary scaffolding. The spatial position on the circumplex carries the truth that a single word can only approximate — so the entry is always complete, and words become aids for building vocabulary through recognition over time, not gates to completing a check-in.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** The first-time emotional tracker — someone who wants to start paying attention to their emotional states but doesn't yet have the vocabulary. They're hiring Emotions Wheel to make the habit of emotional check-in low enough friction to actually stick.

## Key metrics

- **Check-in frequency** — avg check-ins per active user per week; the primary habit signal
- **Session completion rate** — % of initiated sessions that result in a recorded entry; measures whether the approach is delivering on its friction promise
- **Recognition breadth** — distinct emotion words tapped per user, tracked over time; signals whether vocabulary is actually growing

## Tracks

### Habit formation

Making the check-in ritual easy to initiate and complete consistently.

_Why it serves the approach:_ A spatial-first model only reduces friction if users actually return to it — habit formation is where the approach succeeds or fails.

### Spatial interaction model

The coordinate-first field: gestures, pin planting, field-tray coordination, and the visual feedback loop that makes position-recording feel natural.

_Why it serves the approach:_ The coordinate is the primary record; the interaction model must make recording that coordinate feel as low-cost as a thought.

### Vocabulary scaffolding

Recognition pills, word density and coverage on the field, and eventually free-text entry for users who've built their own vocabulary.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Words are scaffolding, not gates — but scaffolding only works if the right words are surfaced at the right time.

### Reflection surface

The history view and how past entries are presented back to the user over time.

_Why it serves the approach:_ The value of the record compounds over time; the reflection surface is where that compounding becomes visible.

## Not working on

- Clinical or diagnostic framing — no scoring, accuracy requirements, or anything implying the user needs to "get it right"
- Social features — no sharing, comparison, or community layer; the record is for the user only
